Instructions:

The game is dealt like a regular Blackjack style structure where two cards are dealt to the player face up and two cards to the computer; one card face down and another card facing up. Using the Baccarat numbering system, the object of the game is to have a hand higher than the opponent as close to 9 as possible. Individual cards are scored as follows: Aces (A) count as 1. Tens and face cards count as 0 (zero). 2 through 9 are counted according to their numerical value. If the total of the cards is a two digit number, the last digit counts as the value of the hand (For Example: 8 + 5 = 13, the hand value is 3). Lucky 9 are a two-card total of 9 without any tens or face cards (Examples: Ace and 8, 2 and 7, 3 and 6, 4 and 5) and all pays 3 to 2. - Lucky 9 ties with a Natural 9 (a 9 plus any tens or face cards). - Lucky 9 win against all three-card totals of 9. - A winning Natural 9 pays even money and ties with any hand totals of 9. Players not dealt a Lucky 9 have the option to Hit a third card or Stand. The players are offered to Split pairs by making a wager equal to the amount of their original wager. Players may split pairs up to three times for a total of four separate hands and will only receive one additional card per hand. Goodluck and Enjoy!
